Gajar Ka Halwa

Description

Carrot Halwa, also known as Gajar ka Halwa or “Gajrela”, is a traditional North Indian dessert. It's made by simmering fresh grated carrots with full fat milk, sugar, and ghee. This delicious dessert is delicately scented with cardamom powder and garnished with chopped nuts.

The dish gets its name “Gajar Halwa” since carrots are known as “Gajar” in Hindi and Halwa is a pudding-like dessert from the Indian Sub-Continent. So Gajar Halwa is essentially an Indian carrot pudding. However, it's not the pudding you are probably thinking of! This neither has an egg nor a thickening agent/ingredient.

The dish can be served in many different ways. One common presentation is the “molded bowl” method, where cooks mash a portion of the food into a container, then turn it upside down so that the food on the plate has a neat, consistent shape. Other dishes are presented at the bottom of a serving bowl, with various colorful garnishes. Some cooks arrange small garnishes in star shapes or other patterns on the surface of the carrot halwa.

Ingredients

  • 2 tablespoons ghee or oil
  • 2 pounds carrots, grated
  • 1 ½ quarts whole milk
  • 2 cups white sugar
  • ½ cup raisins or sultanas
  • 1 ½ teaspoons ground cardamom
  • 1 pinch saffron threads
  • 8 tablespoons chopped pistachio nuts

Steps

  1. Heat ghee in a large saucepan over medium heat and add carrots. Cook and stir briefly, about 3 minutes.
  2. Pour in milk and increase heat; bring to a boil and cook, stirring constantly, for 5 minutes.
  3. Reduce heat to low and simmer gently, uncovered, and stirring occasionally until moisture evaporates, about 1 hour.
  4. Bring carrot mixture back to a boil; add sugar, raisins, cardamom, and saffron.
  5. Cook and stir until mixture has thickened, about 10 minutes.
  6. Ladle carrot halwa into 8 individual serving dishes.
  7. Top each serving with 1 tablespoon pistachios. Serve warm or chill before serving.
